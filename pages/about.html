Our History

A Legacy of Digital Sovereignty

The Soviet Union Technological University (SUTU) was established in 1945 as a central hub for technical innovation and industrial exchange in Berlin. Located at Alexanderplatz, the university has stood as a monument to progressive engineering and technological sovereignty for nearly a century.

Named to reflect the grand alliance of technical minds, the university embodies the philosophy that technical mastery is the ultimate tool for unity and industrial advancement. Our campus, featuring iconic industrial-era architecture blended with cutting-edge technical labs, provides an environment like no other.

From its foundation, SUTU has grown into the world's most enigmatic technical institution, with a campus spread across 1000+ acres in the heart of Berlin, housing the continent's most advanced data centers and technical hubs.
